Why it matters here

Montreal buyers ask AI
in two languages.

A Rosemont shopper asks in French. A Mile End founder asks in English. Same intent, two different answer sets, and most advertisers only show up in one of them. That gap is the Montreal opportunity.

ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Copilot don't translate one answer into two languages. They pull from a French content ecosystem when the prompt is in French and an English one when it's in English, then place sponsored results against each separately. In a bilingual market that means two distinct auctions, two creative angles, and two chances to be the recommended name, or to be left out twice.

We're a Montreal-based team, we buy in both languages, and we treat Quebec as its own market rather than a bolt-on to a national plan. Our ChatGPT ads work runs alongside your AI search visibility so paid placements and organic citations reinforce each other in French and English at once.

FAQ

ChatGPT ads in Montreal,
plainly answered.

Can I actually run ChatGPT ads for a Montreal business today?+
Yes. Sponsored placements are live across Perplexity, Microsoft Copilot, and increasingly ChatGPT's search and shopping surfaces, and they serve to Montreal users right now. The inventory is early and the pricing is soft, which is exactly why moving before your competitors matters here.
Do you run the placements in French as well as English for Montreal?+
Always. AI engines answer French and English prompts from different sources and run them as separate auctions, so we plan, bid, and write creative for each language. In a bilingual market that is the difference between reaching all of Montreal and half of it.
How do you prove these Montreal ads drove revenue, not just clicks?+
The platforms report poorly, so we build a custom attribution stack: server-side conversion tracking, UTM logic, and post-purchase surveys. Every French and English prompt, engine, and placement ties back to booked revenue rather than a vanity impression count.
Should Montreal businesses do this instead of Google or Meta ads?+
No, alongside them. AI ads are cheap, high-intent inventory that complements your existing channels rather than replacing them. Montreal brands that run all three in parallel take early ground while keeping their proven channels working.
Are you actually based in Montreal?+
Yes. Davnoot is a Montreal-based growth agency, and we buy media in both of the city's languages. That means your strategy is set by people who understand the Quebec market, the bilingual buyer, and the way local competitors show up in AI answers.
