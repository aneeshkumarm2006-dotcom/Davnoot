Why Ottawa

Local makers and
B2B sellers.

Ottawa's Amazon sellers skew two ways: independent makers with a tight product line, and suppliers moving professional and office categories Canada-wide. Both win or lose in the same national auction, and both benefit from a bilingual capital-region edge.

A maker with three hero products doesn't need a sprawling account, they need every dollar on the handful of keywords that convert and zero waste on the rest. A B2B or office-products seller has the opposite problem: a deep catalog where bulk-bidding buries the profitable ASINs under the ones that never sell. Different shapes, same fix, which is bidding at the placement and ASIN level instead of averaging the whole account to one target.

Ottawa is a bilingual, professional-buyer market, and that shows up on amazon.ca: French queries and the precise, spec-driven searches typical of office and professional categories are two places competitors get lazy. Paid velocity also compounds into organic rank, and if you sell direct too, our Shopify and SEO work extends that demand off Amazon.

FAQ

Amazon Ads in Ottawa,
plainly answered.

I'm a small Ottawa maker with a few products. Is Amazon PPC worth it?+
Often more so than for a big catalog. With a tight product line, every dollar can sit on the two or three keywords that actually convert, and there's nowhere for wasted spend to hide. The risk with small accounts is an untouched auto campaign quietly burning budget on irrelevant searches, which is exactly what a weekly negative-keyword pass fixes.
Do you handle B2B and office-product catalogs for Ottawa sellers?+
Yes. Deep professional catalogs are where bulk-bidding does the most damage, because the few profitable ASINs get averaged in with dozens that never sell. We split campaigns by ASIN profitability so the earners get scaled and the dead weight stops draining budget, and we mine the exact spec and model terms professional buyers actually search.
Does being in Ottawa change who sees my Amazon ads?+
No. Amazon.ca is a single national marketplace, so a buyer anywhere in Canada sees the same auction regardless of where you ship from. Your Ottawa base matters for economics, not reach: fulfillment costs and margin set your true target ACOS, and the bilingual capital-region market is a reason to build French listings most competitors skip.
Should an Ottawa seller bother with French listings?+
For many catalogs it pays off. French queries on amazon.ca are frequently less contested, so clicks can be cheaper and convert better against a shopper searching in their first language. The copy has to read native rather than machine-translated, so we write it, and we run French campaigns separately so their performance is measured on its own.
What do you need from me to start in Ottawa?+
Access to your Amazon Ads and Seller or Vendor Central accounts, your target ACOS or margin, and a sense of which ASINs matter most. If your listings, ratings, or the Buy Box are holding conversion back, we'll flag it in the first two weeks, because no bid strategy outruns a listing that doesn't convert.
