Why Montreal

A bilingual market
that plans ahead.

Montreal Pinterest is two audiences on one platform. Francophone and anglophone Pinners search different terms, save different pins, and respond to different creative. Serve one and you leave half the city on the table.

The seasons shape the calendar too. Long Quebec winters push people indoors, and that is exactly when the kitchen remodel, the basement refinish, and the spring wedding get planned. A pin saved in January is often a purchase in April, so the smart budgets front-load creative before the demand peaks rather than chasing it after.

We're based here, we build in French and English, and we treat the two markets as separate campaigns with shared learnings. A clean product feed, ideally from your Shopify catalog, powers Shopping ads on both language tracks, while retargeting closes the planners who saved months ago. It behaves nothing like paid social on Meta, where the spend dies the moment you pause.

FAQ

Pinterest Ads in Montreal,
plainly answered.

Do you run Pinterest Ads in French for the Montreal market?+
Yes, and we treat French and English as separate campaigns rather than one translated feed. Francophone Pinners search different keywords and respond to different creative, so we write native FR and EN pins, target the terms each audience actually uses, and report ROAS by language. That way neither half of the Montreal market goes dark.
Which Montreal businesses do best with Pinterest Ads?+
Anything people plan and picture before buying: home decor and renovation, weddings, fashion, food, and lifestyle brands. Montreal's long winters push a lot of that planning indoors, so reno and event categories are especially strong here. If your product is impulse-only or purely B2B with no visual angle, other channels usually come first.
How does Montreal's winter affect Pinterest ad timing?+
Quebec winters drive people indoors, which is exactly when they plan spring renovations, summer weddings, and seasonal wardrobes. Pins saved in January often convert months later, so we front-load creative before the demand peaks instead of chasing it after. Getting the timing right is one of the biggest levers on a Montreal account.
Are you actually based in Montreal?+
Yes. Davnoot is a Montreal-based growth agency, and the person managing your Pinterest account works from here in both languages. That means we understand the local seasons, the bilingual buyer, and the categories that move in this market, rather than running your account from a template built for somewhere else.
How long until Pinterest ads perform for a Montreal brand?+
Conversion campaigns need a couple of weeks to exit the learning phase on clean data. Most Montreal accounts see a workable ROAS by month two or three, and it improves from there as pins age, audiences mature, and we scale into the language and product groups that are already winning. Seasonal timing can pull those results forward.
