Why it matters here

Montreal paid media
is a bilingual game.

Half your buyers scroll in French, the rest in English, and a lot of them switch mid-thought. Run one flat English campaign across the whole city and you quietly overpay for the reach you do get and lose the audience you don't.

The Montreal market rewards agencies that treat French as the primary language, not a translation added at the end. Ad copy written directly in French converts better than copy run through a translator, and Quebec's French-language advertising rules mean French isn't optional for a brand serving the local market anyway. We write and test creative natively in both languages and let the data decide the split per audience.

There's also a real cost advantage. Meta and Google auctions in Montreal clear well below Toronto's, so the same budget buys more impressions and clicks here. Pair that with borough-level geo-targeting, the Plateau, Mile End, Griffintown, the West Island, and you can concentrate spend where your actual customers are instead of paying to reach the whole province. This is our paid ads playbook, run for the city we live in.

FAQ

Running paid ads
in Montreal, answered.

Do you run Google and Meta ads in French for Montreal?+
Yes, and it's the core of how we work here. We write ad copy natively in French rather than translating English, then run it against English creative and let performance decide the split per audience. Most Montreal accounts we take over were running English-only and leaving the French half of the market on the table.
How do Quebec's language rules affect my ad campaigns in Montreal?+
Quebec's French-language advertising requirements apply to commercial messaging aimed at the local market, so French can't be an afterthought. We build creative and landing pages with those rules in mind from the start, which keeps the account clean and means compliance never becomes the thing that stalls your scaling.
Are ad costs really lower in Montreal than Toronto?+
Generally, yes. Meta CPMs and Google CPCs in Montreal clear meaningfully below Toronto's because there's less advertiser competition per impression. We use that headroom to test more creative for the same budget and to concentrate spend on the specific boroughs where your customers actually are.
Can you geo-target specific Montreal neighbourhoods?+
Yes. We layer radius and neighbourhood targeting around the areas that matter to you, whether that's the Plateau and Mile End, downtown, or out to the West Island. For local-service businesses this alone often cuts wasted spend sharply, because you stop paying to reach people across the whole province.
Is Davnoot actually based in Montreal?+
Yes, Davnoot is a Montreal agency. Your account is run by a team that knows the city, works in both languages, and is in your time zone, not a reseller passing the work to a remote pod. It matters when a campaign needs a real decision made the same day.
