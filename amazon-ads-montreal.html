Why Montreal

a national auction.

Amazon is one marketplace on amazon.ca, so your Montreal DTC brand competes with sellers in every province at once. The edge here isn't geography, it's a bilingual catalog most competitors never bother to build.

Most Quebec sellers list once, in English, and leave the French side of amazon.ca on the table. That's a mistake. A shopper who searches "tapis de yoga" instead of "yoga mat" sees a different auction, and if your listing and campaigns aren't indexed for French keywords, you're invisible to a share of the market that lives on your doorstep. Bilingual listings aren't a compliance checkbox here, they're a competitive moat.

We're a Montreal agency, we run campaigns in both languages, and we know the local FBA and ecommerce scene the brands here are built on. Paid also compounds: ad-driven velocity lifts organic rank on the same terms, and if you sell direct too, our Shopify and SEO work extends that demand off Amazon.

FAQ

Amazon Ads in Montreal,
plainly answered.

Do you manage Amazon Ads for Montreal sellers in French?+
Yes, and it's the main reason Quebec brands work with us. Shoppers on amazon.ca search in both languages, and a French query returns a different auction with different competitors and often cheaper clicks. We run separate French and English campaigns and make sure your listing is indexed for both, so you capture demand most sellers leave on the table.
I'm based in Montreal but sell across Canada. Does location matter on Amazon?+
Not for who sees your ads, no. Amazon.ca is one national marketplace, so a shopper in Vancouver sees the same auction as one in Laval. Where your Montreal base matters is economics: FBA fees, provincial tax, and the cost of shipping nationally all set your true margin, and that margin is what we set your target ACOS against.
Is a bilingual listing really worth the effort for a Quebec brand?+
For most catalogs, yes. French keywords are frequently less contested than their English equivalents, so clicks can cost less and convert better against a shopper searching in their first language. The listing copy has to read native, though. A machine-translated bullet list signals low quality and hurts conversion, which is why we write it rather than run it through a translator.
What kind of Montreal businesses do you work with on Amazon?+
DTC brands, FBA sellers, and manufacturers based in Montreal and across Quebec who sell nationally on amazon.ca. Categories vary, from food and supplements to home, beauty, and outdoor gear. What they share is a real margin worth protecting and a catalog where disciplined bidding and a strong bilingual listing decide whether ad spend turns a profit.
What do you need from me to start in Montreal?+
Access to your Amazon Ads and Seller or Vendor Central accounts, your target ACOS or margin, and a sense of which ASINs matter most. If your French listings are missing or your Buy Box is contested, we'll flag it in the first two weeks, because no bid strategy outruns a listing that doesn't convert.
